chevron_rightLNG projects will source natural gas from northeast British Columbia – how is that aspect of the industry regulated?

Natural gas extraction activities, also known as upstream development, is regulated by the BC Oil and Gas Commission.

The BC Oil and Gas Commission is an independent, single-window regulator for the natural gas industry, overseeing the lifecycle of every project, including exploration, development, consultation, pipeline transportation and reclamation.

All permitting and oversight authorities are delegated to the BC Oil and Gas Commission through the Oil and Gas Activities Act – British Columbia’s regulatory framework for all oil and gas activities in the province.

More details about the Commission can be found at www.bcogc.ca.

chevron_rightHow are workforce accommodations regulated?

There are multiple government agencies involved with regulating workforce accommodations.

It starts with the environmental assessment process that assesses environmental, economic, health, cultural/heritage and social effects – and that includes workforce accommodations. Conditions can be imposed on a project’s environmental assessment certificate to add specific regulatory requirements related to the workforce.

The BC Oil and Gas Commission permits the use of Crown Land where the accommodations are built. Northern Health approves construction, food services, drinking water and other necessities. Local governments have a role too by providing permits linked to the BC Building Code – provincial regulation that governs how new constructions are completed.

Other agencies with a role can include the Ministry of Environment and Climate Change Strategy, Ministry of Transportation, Technical Safety BC, and WorkSafeBC.

chevron_rightWill Coastal GasLink’s pipeline transport oil or other products?

The pipeline will only transport raw natural gas. In fact, pipelines built to supply natural gas to a LNG facility in British Columbia can not be converted or permitted to transport other products, including oil and bitumen.
